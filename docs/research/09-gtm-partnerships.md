# LiverRa — Go-to-Market & Partnerships

## The Brutal Reality

Medical-AI sales cycle into a European hospital is 12-24 months. With €1M pre-seed, LiverRa cannot run a traditional direct-sales playbook. **Year 1 = evidence manufacturing. Year 2 = pilots-to-paid. Year 3 = scale.**

**Realistic year-1 targets:**
- 3-5 design-partner sites (mostly unpaid)
- 1-2 peer-reviewed publications submitted
- CE MDR Class IIa pathway started
- 1-2 marketplace partnerships signed
- Revenue: **€50-150k (NOT €500k)**

---

## Adoption Barriers That Kill Deals

| Barrier | Reality | LiverRa counter-move |
|---|---|---|
| **Point solutions don't sell** | Procurement refuses to negotiate 15 vendor contracts; IT refuses 15 app integrations | Position LiverRa as a suite (seg + volumetry + planning + reporting); OR ride a marketplace |
| **Pilot-to-production cliff** | 70% of healthcare AI pilots never scale; models drop 95%→70% accuracy in real PACS data | Federated validation across Regensburg + Potsdam + Geo Hospitals BEFORE paid deployment |
| **No billing code** | No CPT/NTAP = no hospital ROI story; Medicare denied >50% of 93,000 AI claims 2018-23 | Sell on **surgeon time savings** (segmentation 14.9→4.9 min, 67% faster) + OR time + avoided re-ops |
| **Radiologist skepticism** | Only 41% think AI tools address their real problems; fear of replacement | Position as **surgeon-facing**, not radiologist-facing; radiologist reads scan, LiverRa builds surgical map |
| **Liability ambiguity** | EU AI Act + MDR: radiologist must remain decision-maker; who signs? | Design as **decision support** reviewed/approved by surgeon; CE Class IIa; post-market surveillance plan (ESR 2025) |
| **IT integration friction** | PACS, RIS, EHR, OR-planning all siloed | DICOM-native + IHE AI Results profile + on-prem container option |
| **Unclear ROI** | "Research results aren't enough" — CFO wants a number | Health economics white paper with Regensburg Month 6-9 (OR-time saved × €/min; R1-resection × avoided readmission) |

### Breakthrough lessons
- **Aidoc** (17 FDA clearances, 1,200+ hospitals): built a **platform** not tool; anchored on ER workflows where minutes = dollars; engaged AI governance committees (not just radiologists)
- **Viz.ai** (2,000 US hospitals, 230M+ lives): spent 18 months + millions convincing CMS for NTAP — single biggest commercial lever. No European equivalent. Real business value was **increased thrombectomy DRG upcoding** (~50-60% revenue boost)

**LiverRa cannot follow Viz.ai path (no reimbursement pathway in EU for this). Must win via KOL-driven clinical evidence + PACS marketplace distribution.**

4. **Expansion** — transplant / ablation / follow-up modules

## Competitive Displacement Playbook

### vs Visible Patient (paid incumbent service bureau)
- **Speed:** in-house minutes vs 24-48h turnaround
- **Sovereignty:** hospital data stays local (big in DACH)
- **Integration:** DICOM-native in PACS vs external workflow
- **Cost:** per-site subscription beats per-case at >30 cases/month
- Don't attack directly — position as "complement for high-volume HPB wanting in-house capability"

### vs 3D Slicer (free)
Not a price fight — a labor + liability fight:
- Not CE/FDA cleared → not defensible under MDR
- Surgeon time spent = €50-150/hour × 15 min × volume = your real cost frame
- No support, no validation, no audit trail → liability under EU AI Act
- **Pitch:** "3D Slicer saves €0 software cost + loses €X/year surgeon time + creates unquantified regulatory risk"

### vs Fujifilm Synapse 3D (PACS built-in)
- Fujifilm module is geometry-only; LiverRa is surgical-decision-grade
- Position as **complement**, not competitor — integrate via DICOM send
- Year 3 risk: Fujifilm acquisition/exclusion — have Siemens channel ready

# LiverRa — Market Sizing & Funding Comparables

## TAM / SAM / SOM

### TAM — Total Addressable Market (2028)
- Radiology AI 2028 ≈ $1.6B (24.5% CAGR from $0.76B 2025 per MarketsandMarkets)
- Liver = ~8-12% of body-region AI demand → 10% = **$160M**
- Surgical planning 2028 ≈ $155M; liver/HPB = 8% → **$12M**
- **TAM ≈ $172M globally (radiology AI liver + HPB planning) by 2028**

### SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market (DACH + CEE + ME)
- Target regions capture ~18% of global radiology AI spend (DACH 12%, CEE 2%, ME 4%)
- $172M × 18% = **~$31M SAM by 2028**
- Bottom-up sanity check: ~1,200 addressable hospitals × $25k ACV = $30M ✓

### SOM — Realistic 5-year Capture
Benchmark: Aidoc (8 yrs, $370M raised) ~2,000 hospitals globally. Qure.ai (7 yrs, $123M) ~3,000+ sites. Pre-seed LiverRa at year 5 with €15-25M total raised ≈ 80-150 hospitals.

| Bear | 40 × €20k | €0.8M ARR |
| Base | 100 × €30k | €3M ARR = 10% of SAM |
| Bull | 200 × €40k | €8M ARR (Series B material) |

## Liver Disease Burden (Demand Driver)

- **Liver cancer (HCC 80% of primary):** 870,000 new cases 2022 → **1.52M in 2050** (Lancet Commission 2025)
- China alone: ~367,000 new cases 2022; projected 1M/year by 2025
- Fastest-rising etiology: **MAFLD/NAFLD** offsets HepB/HepC decline
- **Metastatic liver disease:** ~50% of colorectal cancer patients develop liver mets → #1 volume driver for HPB planning in Europe (CRLM >> primary HCC)
- **Liver cirrhosis:** ~1.5M deaths/year (GBD); 6-month surveillance imaging in cirrhotics = massive recurring volume

## Bottom Line

TAM is real ($172M by 2028, largest under-served radiology AI vertical after chest/brain). SAM for target markets is $31M — can capture 10% at year 5 = $3M ARR → Series A-stage. Direct competitors are over-priced service bureaus (Visible Patient) or niche fibrosis plays (Perspectum) — whitespace for AI-native HPB surgical planning exists. **€1M pre-seed is tight but credible IF stacked with EIC + EIT Health.** 5-year exit corridor: **$80-250M strategic** to Guerbet, Bayer (Calantic refugee), Olympus/Ziosoft.
